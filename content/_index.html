I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at Mississippi State University, an IZA Research Affiliate, and an Annenberg Institute (Brown University) EdWorkingPapers Contributor. My research interests are in applied microeconomics, including education, labor, crime, history, and health. My research is published in journals including Journal of Public Economics, Journal of Human Resources, and Economics of Education Review. My CV is available here.

I received a Ph.D. in Economics from Cornell University, and a Bachelors in Economics and Masters in Statistics from Utah State University. Before beginning my current position, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Georgia State University’s Georgia Policy Labs. I enjoy making data visualizations; see some examples here.
